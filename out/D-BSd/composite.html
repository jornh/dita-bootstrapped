Opinionated

DITA-Bootstrapped prioritize the reader over the author. We also choose modern web as first class citizen over other kinds of output.

Fresh start:
  • Minimalist approach to navigation (content and context over clicks) - taking advantage of the fact that most screens are more than 1024x768 today
    Note DITA-Bootstrapped still works well on smaller screens like phones or if you resize your browser window to e.g. look at help content side-by-side with the application you are trying to figure out how to operate. How? Well, we take advantage of the responsive features of modern HTML5 CSS styling provided by Bootstrap.
  • Goal oriented by assembling complete solutions on a page by chunking a sequence of closely related tasks. (e.g. a full process) ... XXX and it saves a lot of navigation (a.o. fewer pages to mention in a toc)/fewer related links (sibling links - parent links on same page)
  • XXXXX avoid to long lines hard to follow - there is a reason newspapers have columns XXXX
  • DITA - the good parts
  • Presentation is part of delivery of the message - "clean up the mess" - need to work with whitespace
  • ??? XXX even though DITA very much tries to accommodate for every kind of output - we make choices. And anyway is it really of interest to the user to have the same content in 5 flavors just because it's easy to push a button? "Middle of the road compromises/lowest common denominator" - better to XXXX
These points does yield some extra steps for the author to think about and perform - compared to just delivering a 1:1 topic-to-page output - but to the benefit of the reader. DITA-Bootstrapped tries to provide a combination of tools, conventions and guidance to minimize the extra burden.

Experimental

Fresh start -> how: by connecting the dots of existing (DITA, , thoughts from SPFE, blogs around) ... when SPFE grows a bit more mature might switch - unless these efforts (blender picture!)

Fresh start, trying to answer a few questions:
  • To try shake of legacy thinking we turn the dials to the max (for example 3 column content and getting rid of the classical tri-pane layout which tends to end in an overwhelming TOC).
  • How close to to layout of a marketing site does it make sense to push a help site?
  • Is it possible to integrate
  • How many DITA-topics does it make sense to cram into a single web page? ... and when does it help or hinder the overview for different types of users like the novice, the casual user and the expert?
  • Are there typical patterns of DITA topic types that work well together chunked together on one page? Can we help enforce the good ones and ease the author in quickly setting them up and make sure they look good on the page ?
  • Note Please help!
    Your help is needed: I'm just a programmer/user of technical doc with the occasional need to make small help collections and training material. And when I have to it's really just to fill in the gaps in the user-guides I'm left with from the purveyors of software that i need to help my coworkers digest.
  • Standing on the shoulders of others ... HOW? Currently just a small XSLT stylesheet using the ditac + some conventions (that you as a technical writer could help document :-)
  • So what's still needed? Well, lots of maturation, field testing/feedback from experienced people in the #techcomm (and #elearning) area. A Big Hairy challenge is still to see how far this will scale for big help systems.
  • To stay minimal and flexible (because we are still young) we currently makes heavy use of the DITA outputclass attribute.
